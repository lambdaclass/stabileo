### Free or Very Low Cost for Education

| Vendor | Educational Offering |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Bentley Systems** | 50+ apps free for students/educators (STAAD, RAM, etc.) |
| **Autodesk** | Free educational licenses (Robot, Revit, AutoCAD, etc.) |
| **StruSoft** | Full-featured FEM-Design, Dimension, PRE-Stress, WIN-Statik, Jigi - free for students |

| **Dlubal (RFEM)** | Free student versions available |

### Key Observations
- Most major vendors offer free or heavily discounted educational licenses
- Verification of academic status is standard
- Licenses are restricted to non-commercial educational use
- Bentley and Autodesk are the most generous (fully free with no special paperwork)
- CSI requires a more formal process (dean letter)

## 5. Browser-Based / Cloud Structural Engineering Tools

### Fully Browser-Based Platforms

| Tool | Pricing | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **SkyCiv** | $69-$179/month; $1,250-$5,000/year | 100% cloud, mobile app, API, live chat support |
| **ClearCalcs** | $79-$149/month; $790-$1,490/year | Cloud-based, multi-standard (US, EU, AU, NZ, CA) |
| **WeStatiX** | Not publicly listed | Italian startup, cloud FEA, seed-funded ($3.23M) |
| **Simright Simulator** | Free tier available | Import CAD, browser-based FEA |
| **RetainX** | Free | Retaining wall design only |
| **Onshape Simulation** | Part of Onshape subscription | Cloud-native FEA for mechanical design |

### Key Trends in Cloud/Browser-Based Tools
- **Significantly cheaper** than desktop-based incumbents (10-50x cheaper per seat)
- **Lower barrier to entry** for small firms and freelancers
- **Growing adoption** but still niche compared to desktop tools
- **SkyCiv and ClearCalcs** are the clear market leaders in browser-based structural engineering
- **Multi-standard support** is a differentiator (ClearCalcs covers 5 standards)
- **API access** becoming common (SkyCiv, ClearCalcs Ultimate)
- **No installation or IT overhead** -- reduces total cost of ownership

## 6. Structural Engineering Firm Size & Revenue Statistics

### United States -- Engineering Services (NAICS 541330)
- **Total firms (US):** ~38,516 active companies
- **Total employment:** ~1,098,479 people
- **Average employees per firm:** ~28.5

### Firm Size Distribution
- **Most structural engineering firms:** Small, privately owned, 1-30 employees
- **83-94% of A/E firms:** Fewer than 20 employees
- **Firms above 200 employees:** ~6% of firms, but ~33% of industry employment
- **Many structural engineers** hold ownership positions as partners

### Revenue Benchmarks
- **Small firm monthly revenue:** $50,000 - $200,000 (i.e., $600K - $2.4M/year)
- **Median A&E firm revenue (sold firms, 2025):** Growing 16% since 2021
- **Median discretionary earnings (2025):** $451,450
- **Profit margins (top quartile):** 22%+; healthy projects: 15-20%
- **Utilization rate (average A&E firm):** 81% of staff time billed
- **Revenue multiplier (valuation):** 1.5x - 2.8x revenue

### Largest Firms (for context)
- WSP USA: $8.8B revenue
- Industry total (US engineering services): ~$360.6B (2026 estimate)

### ACEC Member Firms
- ACEC represents ~5,500 firms with 640,000+ professionals
- Average ~116 employees per ACEC member firm (skewed by large firms)
